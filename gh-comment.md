Thank you for reaching out. I've reviewed this request and respectfully decline to remove the images. Here's why:

## The EULA sections you cited support reuse

Section 11.5, which you quoted, explicitly states:

> *"by transferring, sharing, sending, submitting, posting, uploading or making available User Generated Content on the internet, you acknowledge that other users may use, copy and archive your User Generated Content and create derivate works."*

This is a blanket acknowledgment that any UGC posted publicly on the internet may be used, copied, and used to create derivative works by other users. That is exactly what has occurred here.

## Copyright ownership belongs to BeamNG GmbH

Per the BeamNG EULA § 1 (Ownership), all artwork, locations, characters, vehicles, visual designs, animations, and audio-visual effects in BeamNG.drive are owned by BeamNG GmbH. In-game screenshots are derivative of BeamNG's copyrighted assets. A server operator capturing a screenshot does not acquire independent copyright over BeamNG's game assets — the creative elements (vehicle models, maps, lighting, textures) all belong to BeamNG GmbH.

Under US copyright law (*Feist Publications v. Rural Telephone Service*, 499 U.S. 340), a work must possess a "modicum of creativity" to be copyrightable. Pointing a virtual camera at someone else's copyrighted 3D scene and pressing a capture key involves minimal creative expression when the underlying creative content is entirely BeamNG's.

## Public distribution on Discord

These images were sourced from a public Discord channel where users voluntarily share in-game screenshots. Posting content publicly on Discord constitutes voluntary public distribution. Discord's Terms of Service grant other users the ability to view and interact with content posted in public channels.

## Fair use (17 U.S.C. § 107)

Even setting aside the EULA's explicit authorization under § 11.5, fair use applies:

- **Transformative purpose**: The screenshots are used as decorative UI backgrounds in a free, open-source content management tool — a completely different purpose than showcasing a BeamMP server.
- **Nature of the work**: In-game screenshots of someone else's copyrighted game engine, not original creative works.
- **Amount used**: Two images out of thirteen ambient backgrounds, used purely as decoration.
- **Market effect**: None. Background images in a free mod manager have zero impact on the market for BeamMP server screenshots.

## This project already attributes IP to BeamNG GmbH

Both `THIRD-PARTY-NOTICES.md` and a `NOTICE` file in the backgrounds directory explicitly attribute all screenshot IP to BeamNG GmbH — the actual rights holder.

## This is not a valid DMCA takedown

This GitHub issue does not constitute a DMCA takedown notice. A proper DMCA notice requires specific legal attestations filed through [GitHub's official DMCA process](https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/content-removal-policies/dmca-takedown-policy), including identification of the specific copyrighted work and a statement under penalty of perjury.
